NBA Pioneers Classic, Sunday Night Basketball doubleheader highlight 10-game Sunday

Celtics claim inaugural NBA Pioneers Classic, Sunday Night Basketball, Allen's historic 40-piece highlight superb Sunday of hoops.

NBA.com Staff

Updated on February 2, 2026 5:46 AM

Jaylen Brown drops 30 points and 13 rebounds to help the Boston Celtics win the first-ever NBA Pioneers Classic.

Enjoy the best of Sunday’s 10-game schedule with the NBA.com live blog, featuring all of the meaningful moments, performances, observations, news, notes and highlights.

  • Pioneers Classic: Paving the way
  • 3 things to know about NBC’s Sunday Night Basketball
  • Last night’s live blog: Embiid’s 40-piece highlights 6-game Saturday
  • Download the NBA App

What we know after Sunday’s games:

  • Boston wins the inaugural NBA Pioneers Classic with a dominant 107-79 showing versus the Bucks.
  • The 2026 NBA All-Star reserves were announced on NBC/Peacock. There are six 1st-time All-Stars that highlight the field, while LeBron James extends his NBA record with a 22nd consecutive selection.
  • The Pistons set a new franchise record with their 53-point thrashing of the Brooklyn Nets, winning 130-77.
  • Playing for the third time in four days due to their Jan. 8 game being postponed because of issues with the court, the Heat take care of business against the Bulls to improve to 2-1 during that stretch.
  • To tip off Sunday Night Basketball on NBC/Peacock, New York outscores Los Angeles 60-44 in the second half to secure their sixth consecutive win.
  • Kawhi Leonard scores 25 points in the Clippers’ 117-93 win over the Suns, extending a career-best streak of games with 20+ points to 27 in the process.
  • Cleveland’s Jarrett Allen becomes the first player in NBA history to tally 40+ points, 15+ rebounds and 5+ assists in under 30 minutes in a win over Portland. It’s also the first 40-point, 15-rebound game in Cavs’ franchise history.
  • SGA (34 pts, 13 ast) and Cason Wallace (27 pts, 7 3PM) combine for 61 points in OKC’s win over Denver to cap Sunday Night Basketball doubleheader.
